The Australian Gender Pay Gap

with other inequalities
in industry, management,
and leadership representation


Over two thirds (67.4%) of male employees have a full-time position, compared to just 40.6% of female employees.


Part-Time workers make up just 10.8% of male employees, while they make up almost a third (31.9%) of female employees.

Women make up just 18.3% of CEOs, and 39.9% of overall management positions.

Women are greatly under-represented in Machinery Operation/Driving, making up 13.2% of employees, and Technician/Trade roles, at 14%.


Women make up almost three quarters (73.8%) of Clerical and Administrative roles, and 72.0% of Community and Personal Service roles.

Women in Key Management Personnel positions earn just AUD$0.76 for every dollar that men make in the same positions.


This adds up to an AUD$89,141 difference in salary per annum compared to men.

Women in Technician/Trades positions earn just AUD$0.74 for every dollar that men make in the same positions.


This adds up to an AUD$28,363 difference in salary per annum compared to men.

The Gender Pay Gap is at its widest in Western Australia, with women earning $0.78 per dollar compared to men working in the state.


South Australia has the smallest Gender Pay Gap, with women earning $0.93 per dollar earned by men.

Women working in the Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services industry earn just $0.74 for every dollar men in the same industry make.


The industry with one of the smallest Gender Pay Gaps is the Public Administration and Safety industry, at $0.92 for every dollar earned by men.

In Australia, the Gender Pay Gap has seen a steady and consistent reduction over time.


The GPG has fallen from $0.75 per dollar in 2014, to $0.80 per dollar in 2020.
